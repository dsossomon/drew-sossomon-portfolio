About My Grades

I am proud of my dedication to learning and personal growth. As someone with cerebral palsy, left hemiplegia, ADHD, and executive functioning challenges, I work hard every day to overcome obstacles that can impact my academic performance.

Last year, I was able to pass Algebra I, which was a significant accomplishment for me. Now, I am in Geometry and Literature I. Both subjects have been difficult, but I always try my best and participate in class whenever I can. I consistently complete my schoolwork and homework in all my classes.

Test-taking is especially challenging for me, even after studying and retaking tests. My test scores often do not reflect how much I understand or the effort I put in, and this affects my grades. In Geometry and Literature I, I sometimes struggle to participate because I may not know the answer after reading the chapter, or I worry about making mistakes in front of the class.

In the past, at my previous school, Wake Prep Academy, I was bullied for giving wrong answers, so my teachers allowed me to share my responses privately after class. I no longer experience bullying at Youngsville Academy, but I still find it hard to participate the same way in Literature I. Although I turn my bellwork sheet in every Friday during the beginning of class, I receive a zero for not participating during class, and my checkmarks on the bellwork sheet don’t seem to help my grade.

Despite these challenges, I remain committed to my education and continue to advocate for myself and seek out strategies that help me succeed. I believe it’s important to remember that grades don’t always tell the full story of a student’s effort, determination, and achievements.

Thank you for taking the time to understand my journey.
