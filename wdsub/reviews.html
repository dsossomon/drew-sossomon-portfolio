Drew's Top Five MEN'S SOCCER GOALKEEPERS

Here is the top 5 best men's soccer Goalkeepers as of 2026.

Tim Howard

Tim Howard aka Timothy Matthew Howard is a USMNT top Goalkeeper who played professionally with Tourette syndrome. He is best known for his incredible caps with the USMNT. During his time in professional soccer he played for Manchester United (2003-2007), but later joined Everton (2006-2016). He is now an analyst with NBC Sports in the English Premier League. Tim Howard is 46 years old as of 2026.

Marc-André ter Stegen

Marc-André ter Stegen is a professional German goalkeeper who plays for FC Barcelona. He was recently loaned to Girona FC for playing time before the 2026 FIFA WORLD CUP. Ter Stegen is 33 years old. He joined FC Barcelona in 2014 and has been a wall for the club ever since.

Manuel Neuer

Manuel Neuer is a professional soccer goalkeeper for Bayern Munich. He is 39 years old and is considered the best goalkeeper of all time. Known for his high shot-stopping and "sweeper-keeper" style, he has been the starting goalkeeper and captain for Bayern for years. He first joined the national team in 2009.

Oliver Kahn

Oliver Rolf Kahn is a former German professional soccer Goalkeeper. He joined FC Bayern Munich in 1994 and retired in 2006. He is known for his intense aggression, commanding presence, and his unique low, forward-leaning goalkeeper stance.

Andrew Sossomon

Andrew Baranson Garner Sossomon, also known as Drew Sossomon, is a WAKE FC 2009 NPL Soccer Goalkeeper. He broke his school record at Youngsville Academy as the only player to make varsity with a disability. Currently recovering from a torn ACL/Meniscus, he is known for his massive save volume (348 saves in 26 matches) and elite penalty-blocking. He is currently a top prospect for college and the MLS.
